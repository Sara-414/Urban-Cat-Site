Urban Cat Coalition is dedicated to helping create a no-kill community by humanely reducing the community cat population in the Milwaukee area through targeted trap-neuter-return (TNR)

It is estimated that there are about 70 million stray cats in the United States alone.

Every year approximately 1.4 million cats are killed in US shelters.

Less than 5% of stray cats are reclaimed from shelters by their owners.

WHAT IS TNR?

Trap-Neuter-Return (TNR) is the only proven method of humanely reducing the cat population.

Community cats are trapped, fixed, vaccinated, and ear tipped. The cats are held in a safe space for recovery before being released back to their neighborhood. They are provided food and shelter by community cat caretakers.

WHAT ARE THE BENEFITS?

  • Prevents unwanted litters from being born on the streets
  • Significantly reduces nuisance behaviors like yowling, fighting, and territory marking
  • Costs taxpayers less to sterilize than to kill community cats
  • Reduces the number of cats entering shelters, thus reducing euthanasia of adoptable cats
  • Keeps mouse and rat populations under control
