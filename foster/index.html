WE NEED FOSTERS LIKE YOU!

What does fostering involve?

When you foster, you agree to take a cat into your home and give them love, care and attention - for a predetermined period of time or until the cat is adopted.

By fostering you are:

  • Giving a cat the time they need to decompress, heal, and prepare for adoption
  • Helping the rescue learn more about the cat's personality so they can find the right adoptive home
  • Socializing the cat to a home environment - getting them used to noises, people, and other pets
  • SAVING A LIFE!

How is fostering for Urban Cat different than fostering for other rescue organizations?

Urban Cat takes in the "gap cats" - cats that fall between the gaps in animal rescue. These are cats that most other organizations will not or cannot take. They are often euthanized in other rescues.
  • Feral mom cats and their kittens
  • Orphaned neonates
  • Spicy kittens needing socialization
  • Injured community cats
  • Shy cats and kittens deemed unadoptable in a busy shelter setting

If you do not have experience with community cats, that is ok, we can train you and provide tools to assist you. Be aware, this will be more work than playing with cute fuzzy kittens. It is challenging, but fulfilling work. You are saving these lives, no question about it. Our fosters are real life heroes!

What kind of space do I need for a foster cat or kittens?

You will need to keep them in a space that is away from your own animals - a bathroom, guest bedroom, office, or even an XL dog kennel work great. We want to be sure any illness does not spread from the foster cat to your personal pets. After a "quarantine" period of at least two weeks, and after deworming treatments, a lot of foster homes will incorporate healthy kitties in to their "general population" to give them more exposure to living life indoors. However, it is important to be prepared to have to keep cats separate for longer than two weeks - in case there is an illness. Please note that we do not routinely test for FeLV/FIV, so we always encourage foster cats to remain separate from your own animals for their entire stay.

What are my responsibilities as a foster for Urban Cat Coalition?

  • Socialize cats to the best of their abilities (we have guides and videos to help)
  • Take cats to vet appointments
  • Track and report vaccinations and medications
  • Give cats provided medications (dewormer, antibiotics, flea meds, etc)
  • Write up a bio for adoption listing on Petfinder
  • Take photos for social media and Petfinder
  • Coordinate in-home meet and greets with potential adopters

As a foster you have the final say when it comes to adopting out your foster kitties. You will know the cat best - so you know what the perfect home will be!

How do I become a foster?

Fill out the foster application below and if you are approved, we will schedule a virtual home visit to talk to you about the types of cats or kittens you are comfortable working with. Please give us 2-5 business days to review your application. If you haven't heard back from us in 2-5 business days, please make sure to check your spam email!
